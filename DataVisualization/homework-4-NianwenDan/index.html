Years To Shown

Introduction: This radial chart visualizes the monthly average of various weather attributes in Washington, D.C., across selected years. Each line on the chart represents the average value for each month, calculated from daily data points, allowing users to easily identify seasonal patterns and compare year-over-year trends. Users can choose from attributes such as average minimum temperature, wind speed, and humidity, and adjust the radial domain/range for more focused analysis.

Category: Combinatorial

Marks: Each line represents the monthly average of the selected attribute, with each point along the line showing the average for a specific month. This emphasizes seasonal trends while reducing the noise of daily fluctuations.

Channels: Different colors distinguish each year, enabling quick visual comparisons of monthly averages across years.

Interactions: Users can select the attribute to visualize, adjust the domain/range of the chart, and choose specific years for comparison, allowing for an in-depth, customized view of weather trends.
